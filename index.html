Systems Serology Mechanistic Tensor Decomposition

In the summer of 2022, I worked with Dr. Aaron Meyer at UCLA through the Amgen Scholars program. I helped to develop a novel mechanistic tensor decomposition incorporating a multivalent, multiligand binding model to model Fc receptor binding. We validated the model on five studies of HIV and SARS-CoV-2 infection immune responses to verify accuracy and model agreement with known IgG isotype properties. I presented this work at the Annual Biomedical Research Conference for Minoritized Scientists (ABRCMS) where it was recognized with a presentation award in the Computational and Systems Biology Category.

Hamro Katha

Growing up as the daughter of Nepali immigrants and having spent the first three years of my life living in Dharan, Nepal I often write and think about my cultural identity. Curious to better understand immigration stories, acculturation, and the preservation of culture through generations I interviewed 30+ Nepali immigrants from around the U.S. in the summer of 2022. Their stories' and journeys' are captured in a project called HAMRO KATHA linked below.

Writing

My writing style of choice is short stories, but I have recently begun dabbling in poetry! I have taken numerous creative writing classes at Stanford in fiction, creative nonfiction, and poetry, and have been able to learn and grow from the mentorship if Jenn Trahan, Rose Whitemore, Brittney Perlham, Jackson Holbert and numerous others. In high school I attended the Iowa Young Writer's Studio and was recognized by the Scholastic Art and writing Awards. I am currently working on a collection of short stories based on the experiences of the Nepali diaspora in the U.S.

"Turmeric Stains", published by Columbia College Chicago, this creative nonfiction short story depicts colorism in South Asian society through the eyes of a young girl.

"The Clock" published in Canvas Literary Jounal's Autumn 2019 Issue, this horror short story tells the tale of a young boy, his mother, and a fateful last night togther.
