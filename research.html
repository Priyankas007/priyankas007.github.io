Human Physiology: An Overview

Gastrointestinal System

Main Parts of the Gastrointestinal System

Food travels from the mouth to the anus through the following path:
mouth --> upper esophageal sphincter --> esophagus --> lower esophageal sphincter --> stomach --> pancreas --> liver --> phyloric sphincter --> small intestine --> ileocecal sphincter --> large intestine --> internal and external anal sphincters --> anus.

Mouth: mechanical and checmial digestion/lubrication of food
Esophagus: temporarily stores food, initiates digestion by churning and secreting proteases and acid
Pancrease: accessory gland; secrets crucial enzymes and HCO3- into tract
Liver: accesory gland; secrets bile into gallbladder
Small Intestine: composed of three parts: (1) Duodenum, (2) Jejunum, (3) Ileum ("DJ Ileum"); primary site of nutrient and water absorption
Large Intestine: reabsorbs fluid and electrolytes; stores fecal matter

There is some variation in the anatomy of the walls of th GI tract, but certain features are shared. Here are the most important shared features from inside to outside.
Muosa: consists of an epitheial layer, a layer of loose connective tissue (lamina propria) that contains capillaries, enteric neurons, and immune cells, and a muscle layer (muscalaris mucosa); most important for absorptoin in the small intestine.
Submucocsa: interchange of muscles and glands that secrete material into the lumen
Muscularis externa: composed of inner circular muscle layer (for chopping) and longitudinal muscle layer for movement; contains enteric neurons
Serosa: outer covering in abdomen, appends it to peritoneum
